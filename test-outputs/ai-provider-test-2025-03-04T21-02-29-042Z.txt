Prompt: Provide a helpful and friendly response to this customer question: How do I find the right running shoe size?

Response: Finding the right running shoe size is crucial for comfort and performance. Here's a simple guide to help you:

1. Measure your foot: You can do this at home using a ruler or tape measure. Measure from the back of your heel to the tip of your longest toe. Do this for both feet as it's not uncommon for one foot to be larger than the other. Use the larger measurement for choosing your running shoe size. 

2. Consider the width: Running shoes also come in different widths. If your feet are wider or narrower than average, you might need a special shoe size. 

3. Your running shoes should be larger than your dress shoes: This is because your foot expands when you run. A good rule of thumb is to have a thumb's width of space between the end of your longest toe and the end of the shoe.

4. Try shoes in the afternoon or after a run: Your feet swell throughout the day and after exercise. Trying on running shoes when your foot is at its largest will help ensure a good fit.

5. Test the shoes: Wear the socks you normally wear when running. Try both shoes on and jog or run around the store a bit to check for comfort.

Remember, everyone's feet are unique and the most important thing is that the shoes feel comfortable. Don't rely solely on the shoe size, pay attention to the overall fit and feel of the shoes. If you're still unsure, a specialist running store can provide expert advice and fitting services.
